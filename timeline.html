The Framework

A valid solution architecture requires three structures: a model for how to instruct AI, two pillars that make the mind-to-mathematics mapping feasible, and the one contribution that must always remain human.

I · The Blank Slate

Learns from human records of play. Performance ceiling is locked to the quality of that historical data. "Garbage in, garbage out."

AlphaZero — Rules Only
Input
Foundational rules of Go
↓
Outcome
Surpassed millennia of expertise

Receives only the rules, derives optimal strategy through self-play. Unconstrained by the quality of prior human attempts.

The framework follows the same logic: provide AI not with pre-concluded moral answers, but with the rules of the game — uncontaminated by the historical limits of human moral systems.

II · The Two Pillars

III · The Human Role

If AI can learn all rules and derive all optimal solutions autonomously, what remains irreducibly human? The answer rests on two strictly distinguished concepts.

𝒊 Intentionality
·
τ Telos
=
→v The Value Vector

Intentionality — the structural directedness of consciousness toward objects — draws the arrow. Telos — the ultimate completion state — defines the direction. Together: the only input that cannot be delegated.

Without the vector

Without a subject whose intentionality marks one direction as "I want this" and another as "I do not want that," the universe has no value axis. Stars collapse and life emerges with equal meaninglessness — or equal grandeur. The lamp has infinite power. It acts only when a subject speaks the wish.

The human as first mover

Humanity's singular, non-transferable role is that of the prima causa: providing the initial directional impulse from which all subsequent derivation proceeds. The task is not to solve the problem. The task is to define the endpoint with sufficient precision that the system capable of solving it can hear the instruction.
