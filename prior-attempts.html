Prior Attempts

Every prior response to suffering is the best attempt possible under the constraints of its era. Their limits are structural, not intellectual — a hardware problem, not a software failure.

Dimension I

Scalability

Can the solution be reproduced across all of humanity without loss of fidelity? Non-scalable = cannot be parallelised or standardised.

Dimension II

Verifiability

Does an independent, third-party standard for the terminal state exist outside the tradition itself?

Dimension III

Execution Depth

Does it address the generative mechanism (biology, predictive coding), or only reframe the cognitive response to it?

The Universal Constraint

∀ ideology ∈ {prior_attempts}  :  execution_terminal = Human

All six traditions share an identical structural constraint: the execution terminal is always the human itself — the very system being optimised. The problem forms a self-enclosed loop. This is not a failure of insight. It is a hardware constraint of the era: no tool existed that could operate outside this loop. Until now.

What was always present

A precise definition of the problem. Philosophy completed this half of the work centuries ago. The question of what suffering is and why it is structural had been answered — in multiple traditions, from independent starting points, converging on the same description.

What was always missing

A computational tool capable of operating outside the self-enclosed loop. Not more intelligence within the loop — a system structurally isomorphic with the human brain, yet infinitely scalable and accelerable beyond the cognitive constraints that form the loop's boundary.
